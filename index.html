Ahmedabad, founded on Thursday, the 26th, in the month of February, in the year of 1411 at approximately ~14:20, by Al-Sultan al-A'zam Nasir al-Dunya wa'l-Din Abu'l-Fath Ahmad Shah I, Sultan of Gujarat on the banks of the Sabarmati River, is the largest city in Gujarat and a major industrial hub. Famous as the former capital of Gujarat, it was dubbed the "Manchester of India" due to its massive textile industry. The city is known for its UNESCO World Heritage Old City status and strong ties to Mahatma Gandhi.

Manek Chowk

Manek Chowk is a bustling historic square in Old Ahmedabad. It changes function throughout the day: vegetable market in the morning, jewellery market in the afternoon, and street food hub at night.

Adalaj Stepwell

The Adalaj Stepwell is a 15th-century architectural marvel known for its detailed carvings and five-storey design blending Indo-Islamic styles.

Sabarmati Ashram

Sabarmati Ashram was established by Mahatma Gandhi in 1917 and played a key role in India’s independence movement, including the Dandi March.

Sabarmati Riverfront

The Sabarmati Riverfront is an 11.5 km urban development with promenades, parks, and recreational spaces along the river.

Sidi Saiyyed Mosque

The Sidi Saiyyed Mosque is famous for its stone lattice windows, especially the iconic Tree of Life carving, built in the 16th century.
